	\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ABSTRACT}
	Large language models (LLMs) are capable of multi-hop reasoning but remain prone to hallucination. Knowledge graph-constrained decoding mitigates this by restricting output tokens to valid paths within a knowledge graph (KG), ensuring absolute structural faithfulness, unlike standard soft-grounding approaches such as Retrieval-Augmented Generation. However, leading constrained paradigms, including Graph-Constrained Reasoning (GCR), construct constraints solely from graph structure. By ignoring the question's semantics and the evolving generation state, these approaches suffer from the \emph{permissiveness problem}: at every decoding step, the allowed valid token set contains thousands of structurally accessible but contextually irrelevant paths. This excess of valid tokens forces the LLM to rely on the very parametric biases that constrained decoding was designed to suppress.

	To resolve this limitation, we propose Dynamic Context-Aware Tries (DCA-Trie), a novel framework that conditions the valid tokens jointly on the knowledge graph, the input question, and the partially generated reasoning chain. This approach upgrades the constraint oracle to an active semantic state $f(\mathcal{G}, E_q, q, y_{<t})$ by using a pretrained relevance scorer to filter candidate KG paths. We develop two distinct architectures: static semantic filtering applied at trie construction (DCA-Trie v1), and dynamic, step-wise bounding conditioned on the evolving generation (DCA-Trie v2).

	Both variants are evaluated on the WebQSP and ComplexWebQuestions datasets over Freebase. Alongside standard evaluating metrics (Hits@1, F1), we introduce the Semantic Irrelevance Ratio (SIR), the first metric designed to uniquely isolate and quantify the proportion of structurally valid but semantically irrelevant paths in a constraint set.
\end{abstract}
